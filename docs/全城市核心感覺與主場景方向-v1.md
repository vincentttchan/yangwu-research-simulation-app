# 全城市核心感覺與圖片 Prompt 總表 v5

用途：作為所有城市主場景、熱點小圖、設施小圖的單一製作文件。此版採用「城市全景張力」標準：主圖要讓玩家感覺整座城市在洋務運動中的角色，而不是只看見一個角落、一間房或一條街。

## 0. 全局視覺標準

所有城市主場景都應接近現有上海圖的成功方向：

- 19th century steel engraving / copper etching
- panoramic city-scale scene
- continuous foreground, middle ground, background
- no labels, no caption boxes, no inset panels, no modern UI
- hotspots embedded naturally inside city life
- four corner facilities do not need to appear in the main image; facility images handle those separately
- horizontal 16:9
- aged paper texture, dense fine crosshatching

### 0.2 北京新版準則：套用到所有城市

北京新版 prompt 的核心不是「把元素放多一點」，而是建立視覺秩序。所有城市主圖都應遵守以下準則：

- 主圖必須有一個清楚的中景主體：例如總理衙門、馬尾船政、北洋軍政核心、威海衛軍港、維多利亞港、十三行、漢陽鐵廠、開平煤礦。
- 前景只用來建立尺度、生活感和進入畫面的路徑，不可變成巨型人物海報、單一物件特寫或舞台式擺拍。
- 熱點元素要被街道、河道、碼頭、山坡、軍港、鐵路等空間動線連接，不可像貼上去的圖示或道具。
- 不使用 cutaway rooms、展示櫥窗、插圖框、標籤牌、局部室內剖面。室內活動如同文館、書局、辦公處，只能以門口、窗內、庭院一瞥或獨立小圖呈現。
- 遠景要承載該城市的歷史壓力：北京是紫禁城，福州是海戰陰影，天津是大沽與北方防務，威海衛是外海威脅，香港是殖民山城，廣州是通商與戰爭記憶，南京是戰後廢墟，武漢是工業地平線，開平是鄉土與礦業衝突。
- 構圖應有「前景入口 -> 中景主體 -> 遠景壓力」的閱讀路徑，讓玩家感到自己正在進入一座城市，而不是看一張歷史元素拼貼。

主場景固定句式：

```text
The image should feel like a panoramic city-scale scene, not a close-up of one street corner.
Foreground...
Middle ground...
Background...
Composition...
The emotional impact should be...
```

## 主場景 Prompt

```text
19th century copper etching, panoramic city-scale view of Shanghai Bund and the Huangpu River, circa 1865-1872. The image should feel like a wide historical port city scene, not a close-up of one dock. Foreground: crowded stone embankment with Chinese coolies carrying bales, cargo crates, ropes, compradores, merchants, tea-house figures, and street traffic, all kept human-scale. Middle ground, main focal point: the Huangpu River as the city artery, crowded with Chinese junks, sampans, and British-style paddle steamers with dark smoke. One pier hints at students and luggage departing for overseas study without becoming a separate display scene. Background: Bund merchant houses, foreign flags, counting houses, and farther industrial smokestacks suggesting Jiangnan Arsenal. Composition: the viewer's eye should move from foreground cargo and workers into river traffic, then toward Bund finance and arsenal smoke. Avoid theatrical staging, oversized foreground figures, cutaway rooms, or objects that look pasted on top of the city. The emotional impact should be cosmopolitan, busy, transitional, and uneasy: old Chinese commerce, foreign capital, and new industrial power sharing the same water. Warm sepia-amber tone, dense crosshatching, aged paper texture, horizontal 16:9, no labels or caption boxes.
```

# 3. 北京 Beijing

## 主圖檔案

`assets/city/city-beijing.webp`

## 主場景 Prompt

```text
19th century steel engraving, panoramic city-scale view of Beijing's Inner City around Dongtangzi Hutong and the Imperial City, 1861-1862. The image should feel like a wide historical political city scene, not a close-up of one courtyard, one official, or one street corner.

Foreground: a broad hutong street with Qing clerks, sedan chairs, document boxes, palace messengers, small groups of officials, and red-sealed memorials being carried toward the central yamen. No single foreground figure should dominate the image; all officials should remain human-scale within the city. A conservative elderly scholar-official holding a memorial scroll stands in partial shadow near the street edge, looking toward the yamen and the school with suspicion, but he is not the main subject.

Middle ground, main focal point: the Zongli Yamen within a cluster of traditional Qing yamen compounds. It should be the clearest architectural anchor in the scene: grey hutong walls, red gates, courtyards, dispatch boxes, treaty documents, clerks, and a visible entrance path. Two Western diplomats in dark frock coats and top hats move along the same street toward the yamen entrance carrying folded treaty documents, while Qing attendants keep a careful distance. The viewer's eye should naturally follow the street from foreground documents to these envoys and into the yamen.

Side middle ground: the Tongwen Guan should appear as a modest converted Qing side compound near the yamen, not as a cutaway display window. Show only glimpses through a doorway or courtyard opening: young banner students, a globe, foreign-language books, brushes, ink, and a simple astronomical instrument. It should feel discovered inside the old administrative city, not exhibited like a museum scene.

Background: the Forbidden City wall and muted yellow imperial rooflines loom beyond the yamen roofs, larger and more silent than everything in the middle ground. The old imperial order should press down on the new institutions.

Composition: one continuous city panorama with depth and flow: foreground officials and documents, middle-ground yamen and foreign envoys, side-ground Tongwen Guan glimpses, far-background Forbidden City walls. Avoid theatrical staging, oversized portrait figures, cutaway rooms, or foreground objects that look pasted on top of the city. The emotional impact should be solemn, compressed, and torn: old imperial authority, new foreign diplomacy, and contested Western learning occupying the same city. Cool stone grey, faded red lacquer, muted imperial gold, dry ink brown, aged paper texture, dense fine crosshatching, horizontal 16:9, no labels or caption boxes.
```

# 4. 福州 / 馬尾 Foochow / Mawei

## 主圖檔案

`assets/city/city-fuzhou.webp`

## 主場景 Prompt

```text
19th century steel engraving, panoramic city-scale view of Mawei harbour on the Min River near Fuzhou, 1866. The image should feel like a Qing naval-industrial town forming along the river, not a scenic tourist harbour, not a close-up of one dock, and not a generic European shipyard. Use a slightly elevated oblique viewpoint, but keep the architecture and labour human-scale.

Foreground: a continuous muddy Mawei riverbank with timber beams, ropes, iron fittings, coal baskets, small sampans, sailors, shipwrights, and workers carrying materials toward the slipways. A few naval academy students with books, a compass, and rolled charts may appear near a modest Fujian-style school building at the edge of the shipyard, but they must remain small within the larger scene. Avoid a large foreground desk, oversized open books, or a posed student portrait.

Middle ground, main focal point: the Foochow Arsenal and Mawei dockyard should be the clearest anchor of the image. Show long low workshop sheds, timber storage yards, a modest administrative building, small brick chimneys, several inclined slipways descending into the Min River, scaffolding, pulleys, rope rigging, and one half-assembled wooden steamship hull. French engineers and Qing officials should stand naturally beside one slipway, reviewing plans with Chinese craftsmen, but the shipyard district must dominate, not the meeting table.

River middle ground: the Min River should connect every part of the scene. Include traditional Fujian junks, bamboo rafts, small working boats, and a few early steam vessels. Keep river traffic busy but not glamorous; it should feel like a working naval river, carrying timber, coal, students, sailors, and warship parts.

Background: humid Fujian hills and white-walled local houses should frame the river. Luoxing Pagoda / China Tower should appear as a smaller navigational landmark on a hill or river bend, not a giant scenic tower. Low coastal batteries and a few distant dark warship silhouettes should sit near the widening river mouth in mist, foreshadowing later sea-defense crises without turning the image into a battle painting.

Composition: the viewer's eye should follow riverbank materials and workers into the slipways, then along the Min River toward the pagoda, coastal batteries, and distant ship shadows. The emotional impact should be ambitious but uneasy: a naval dream being built through labour, study, foreign expertise, and local geography, while the river already points toward future defeat. Avoid theatrical staging, oversized students or engineers, giant foreground documents, cutaway workshops, tourist postcard scenery, bright blue water, or foreground tools that look pasted on top of the harbour. Muted sepia, damp grey-green Fujian hills, restrained dark teal river, warm timber, iron grey, smoke grey, aged paper texture, dense fine crosshatching, horizontal 16:9, no labels or caption boxes.
```
